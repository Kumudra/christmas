Christmas

Christmas is an annual holiday commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ, observed on December 25, with traditions that include gift-giving, decoration, and festive gatherings.

Santa Claus (Saint Nicholas)

Legendary gift-giver, rooted in Christian and European traditions, Santa is a central figure in global Christmas celebrations, known for his iconic red suit and jolly demeanor.

Christmas Tree

Originating in 16th-century Germany, the Christmas tree symbolizes eternal life and joy. Families worldwide gather to decorate trees with lights, ornaments, and a tree topper during the holiday season.

Christmas Carols

Festive songs, from hymns to popular tunes, celebrate the joy, hope, and religious significance of Christmas. Carols are a cherished tradition, performed in churches, homes, and public spaces during the holiday season.

Gift-Giving Tradition

Inspired by the Three Wise Men's gifts to baby Jesus, the Christmas gift-giving tradition symbolizes love and generosity. People exchange gifts with family and friends, embracing acts of charity and goodwill during this festive season.
